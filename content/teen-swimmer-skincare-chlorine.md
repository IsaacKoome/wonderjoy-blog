## ðŸ”Ž Acne, Irritation or Folliculitis?

Ordinary acne includes blackheads and whiteheads and develops gradually. Irritant skin may sting, itch and appear dry or red. Hot-tub folliculitis often appears as itchy, tender, pimple-like bumps after poorly maintained hot-tub exposure, especially under swimwear.

Do not self-treat every rash with acne acids. Stop the suspected exposure and seek advice for spreading pain, fever, pus, raw eczema or a rash that does not improve.

**Does chlorine clear acne?** No. It may temporarily dry oil but can also irritate the barrier.

**Should teens exfoliate after swimming?** Usually not immediately. Rinsing and moisturizing are the priority.

**Can eczema-prone teens swim?** Often yes with precautions, but avoid the pool during raw, infected or severe flares.
