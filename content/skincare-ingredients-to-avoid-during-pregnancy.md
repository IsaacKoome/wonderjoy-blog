Pregnancy is a beautiful journey — but it also comes with many changes, especially to your skin.

While some products may seem harmless, certain skincare ingredients can actually be unsafe during pregnancy. That’s why it’s important to know what to avoid and what to use instead.

---

## ⚠️ Why You Should Be Careful with Skincare During Pregnancy

Your skin absorbs ingredients into your bloodstream. Some of these can potentially affect your baby.

👉 This doesn’t mean you should stop skincare — it just means choosing the **right products**.

---

## ❌ Skincare Ingredients to Avoid During Pregnancy

### 1. Retinoids (Vitamin A Derivatives)

These are commonly found in anti-aging and acne products.

- Retinol  
- Retin-A  
- Tretinoin  
- Adapalene  

👉 These are strongly linked to birth defects and should be avoided completely.

---

### 2. Salicylic Acid (High Concentrations)

Low amounts in cleansers may be safe, but strong treatments and peels should be avoided.

👉 Instead, choose gentler exfoliants.

---

### 3. Hydroquinone

Used for skin lightening and dark spots.

- High absorption rate into bloodstream  
- Not recommended during pregnancy  

---

### 4. Chemical Sunscreens (Some Types)

Ingredients like:

- Oxybenzone  
- Avobenzone  

👉 These may disrupt hormones.
