If you're trying to fade dark spots, you've probably come across two skincare ingredients over and over again:

**Niacinamide** and **Vitamin C**.

Both are backed by research and both can help improve skin tone—but they work in different ways.

So which one should you choose?

Let's compare them side by side.

---

## 🤔 What Is Niacinamide?

Niacinamide is a form of **Vitamin B3** that helps strengthen the skin barrier while improving uneven skin tone.

It may help:

- Fade post-acne dark spots
- Reduce redness
- Control excess oil
- Improve skin barrier function
- Minimize the appearance of pores

It's gentle enough for most skin types, including sensitive skin.

---

## 🍊 What Is Vitamin C?

Vitamin C is a powerful antioxidant that helps brighten the skin and protect it from environmental damage.

Benefits include:

- Brightens dull skin
- Helps fade pigmentation
- Supports collagen production
- Protects against free radicals
- Improves overall skin radiance

Vitamin C works best when applied in the morning alongside sunscreen.

## 🧴 Which One Is Better for Dark Spots?

If your main concern is **post-acne dark spots**, both ingredients can help.

Choose **Niacinamide** if:

- You have sensitive skin.
- Your skin becomes oily easily.
- You also struggle with redness.

Choose **Vitamin C** if:

- Your skin looks dull.
- You want brighter, glowing skin.
- You're targeting sun-related pigmentation.

For many people, using **both** can provide even better results.

## 💡 Can You Use Niacinamide and Vitamin C Together?

Yes.

Older advice suggested separating them, but modern formulations have shown that they can safely be used together by most people.

A simple routine could look like:

**Morning**

1. Gentle cleanser
2. Vitamin C serum
3. Moisturizer
4. Sunscreen

**Evening**

1. Gentle cleanser
2. Niacinamide serum
3. Moisturizer

This approach gives your skin the benefits of both ingredients.

---

## ✨ Final Thoughts

Niacinamide and Vitamin C are both excellent ingredients for treating dark spots.

Niacinamide is a great choice if your skin is oily, sensitive, or easily irritated, while Vitamin C excels at brightening dull skin and improving overall radiance.

Rather than choosing one over the other, many people achieve the best results by using both as part of a consistent skincare routine.

---

> 💡 Tip: No dark spot treatment works well without sunscreen. Daily SPF helps prevent existing spots from becoming darker and protects the progress you've made.
