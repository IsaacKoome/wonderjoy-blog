Diaper rash is common because urine, stool, friction and moisture stay close to the skin. The usual irritant rash often affects exposed areas while sparing deep groin folds. A bright rash involving folds with smaller “satellite” spots may suggest yeast, especially after antibiotics—but similar rashes need different treatment, so photographs are not a diagnosis.

## 🚼 Change Wet or Soiled Diapers Promptly

Frequent changes reduce contact time with urine and stool. During diarrhea, check more often. Choose a well-fitting, absorbent diaper and allow brief diaper-free time on a washable surface when practical.

Do not fasten the diaper tightly over sore skin. Friction and trapped warmth can delay recovery.

## 💦 Clean Without Scrubbing

Use warm water and a soft cloth or squeeze bottle. If wipes sting, switch temporarily to water; when using wipes, choose alcohol-free and fragrance-free. Pat dry or allow air drying rather than rubbing.

Do not try to remove every trace of barrier paste at each change. If it is not soiled, leave the clean layer and add more.

## 🍄 Irritant, Yeast, Bacterial or Allergy?

- **Irritant:** exposed diaper areas, often folds spared
- **Yeast:** may involve folds with smaller spots at the edge
- **Bacterial:** crust, pus, spreading warmth or a sharply red anus can be clues
- **Contact allergy:** repeats where a particular wipe, cream or diaper touches

Do not treat all four the same. Avoid over-the-counter antibiotic ointment unless instructed because some ingredients can worsen contact dermatitis. Do not use adult steroid, antifungal or combination cream on a baby without pediatric directions.

## 🚩 When to Call

Contact the pediatrician if the rash worsens or does not improve after two to three days, or if there are blisters, peeling, pus, open or crusted sores, bleeding, fever or significant pain. Call sooner for a newborn, a baby who seems unwell or a rash that appeared during antibiotics.

## ❓ Quick Questions

**Should I use powder to keep the area dry?** No. Powder can be inhaled and does not create the same protective barrier as paste.

**Must I switch diaper brands?** Not always. Consider a change if the rash repeats in the exact contact pattern after a new diaper or wipe.

**Does teething directly burn the skin?** Teething can coincide with more saliva and stool changes, but persistent rash still deserves ordinary barrier care and assessment when needed.

## ✅ Bottom Line

Frequent changes, water-based gentle cleaning, air drying and a thick appropriate barrier layer help most irritant diaper rash. Fold involvement, worsening or infection signs need pediatric diagnosis.
