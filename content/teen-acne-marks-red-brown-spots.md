A pimple can disappear and leave a flat red, purple or brown reminder for months. These spots are usually post-inflammatory color changes rather than permanent indented scars. Red or purple marks are often called PIE; brown or gray marks are post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation, or PIH.

**Fading begins by preventing the next inflamed pimple.** Treating marks while new acne continues is like drying a floor while the tap is running.

## ðŸ”Ž Marks and Scars Are Different

A flat area of changed color is generally a mark. A dent, raised area or change in skin texture is a scar and is less likely to disappear with an over-the-counter brightening serum. A dermatologist should assess early scarring so further scars can be prevented.

Deeper skin tones develop PIH more readily, and irritation from aggressive treatment can make discoloration darker.

## â³ Use a Months-Not-Days Timeline

Marks often fade gradually over several months, with deeper color taking longer. Photograph once monthly rather than checking every hour. No safe product can erase a spot overnight, and bleaching creams from unverified sellers may contain unsafe or undisclosed ingredients.

## ðŸ©º Know When Professional Care Helps

See a dermatologist for indented or raised scars, continuing painful acne, quickly worsening discoloration or distress that affects school and daily life. Professional treatment should match skin tone because some procedures can worsen pigmentation when used incorrectly.

**Are all dark spots acne scars?** No. Flat color is usually PIH; scars change texture.

**Does lemon juice fade marks?** It can irritate and increase sun sensitivity. Skip it.

**Should teens use hydroquinone?** Only with appropriate medical guidance and a verified product.
