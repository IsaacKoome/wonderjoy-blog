You do **not** need to double cleanse every night. One thorough wash with a gentle cleanser is enough for many people. A two-step cleanse is most useful when you wear heavy makeup, water-resistant sunscreen or products that remain after one wash.

Double cleansing means using an oil-based cleanser, balm or micellar water first, followed by a water-based facial cleanser. It does not mean washing twice with a harsh foaming product.

## 🫧 When One Cleanse Is Enough

Choose one cleanse after a makeup-free day, when your regular cleanser removes sunscreen comfortably, or when your skin is dry and easily irritated. Massage gently with fingertips, rinse with lukewarm water and pat dry.

A tight, squeaky feeling is not proof of cleanliness. It may mean the cleanser or water temperature is too harsh. Cleansing should remove residue without leaving skin burning or flaky.

## 💄 When a First Step Helps

Add a first cleanse when foundation remains around your hairline, mascara survives washing or water-resistant sunscreen leaves a noticeable film. Work the remover over dry skin according to its label, emulsify if required and rinse. Then use a small amount of gentle cleanser.

Micellar water can be a first step, but rubbing repeatedly with cotton pads may irritate sensitive skin. Avoid fragranced wipes as a daily substitute for washing, and never use cooking oils that were not formulated for facial use.

## 🌿 Protect the Skin Barrier

More cleansing is not automatically better for acne. Scrubbing and over-washing can increase irritation without clearing pores faster. After cleansing, apply moisturizer while skin is still comfortable and slightly damp.

## 🧪 Does Double Cleansing Prevent Acne?

It may help when leftover makeup or sunscreen contributes to clogged pores, but it is not an acne treatment by itself. Acne involves oil, clogged follicles, inflammation and other factors. Use proven treatment consistently rather than trying to wash breakouts away.

If a cleansing balm seems to trigger bumps, stop it and return to your previous routine. The individual formula—not oil cleansing as a whole—may be the problem.

## 👁️ Be Gentle Around the Eyes

Hold remover briefly over long-wear eye makeup instead of scrubbing. Keep cleanser out of the eyes and stop if swelling or persistent irritation develops. Contact-lens wearers should follow eye-product directions carefully. Waterproof mascara may need a dedicated remover, but the rest of the face can still use a simpler first step.

## ☀️ Do Not Fear Sunscreen

Do not skip sunscreen because removal feels difficult. Choose a facial sunscreen you can comfortably cleanse at night and use as directed during the day. A good routine makes protection sustainable.

## ❓ Quick Questions

**Should oily skin double cleanse daily?** Only if one cleanse leaves residue. Oiliness alone does not require two steps.

**Can I double cleanse morning and night?** Usually that is unnecessary and may be drying.

**Is cleansing balm comedogenic?** It depends on the full formula and your skin, not one ingredient label.

## ✅ Bottom Line

Double cleansing is a useful removal technique, not a mandatory skincare rule. Use it on heavier-product days and keep both steps gentle.
