Sensitive skin does not automatically ban exfoliation. It does mean you should have a clear reason for doing it and a low-risk plan. If your face is currently burning, peeling, swollen, cracked, or unusually red, **do not exfoliate it**—focus on recovery first.

## 1. Decide Whether You Need It

Skin naturally sheds cells. Exfoliation may help rough texture or clogged pores, but it is optional, not a requirement for healthy skin. A soft, comfortable complexion is a better target than the temporary shine produced by over-polishing.

## 2. Choose One Method

Physical exfoliation removes cells through friction; chemical exfoliation uses acids or enzymes. Rough scrubs, stiff brushes, and aggressive rubbing are easier to overdo. Some sensitive complexions tolerate a mild acid product, but concentration, formula, and frequency matter more than marketing labels.

## 3. Start Once a Week

Patch test first. Use the exfoliant on a calm evening, exactly as directed, then moisturize. Do not combine it that night with a retinoid, another acid, a scrub, or a strong acne treatment. Wait several days and judge your skin—not the calendar—before repeating.

## 4. Watch for the Stop Signs

More stinging, tightness, shiny-but-dry skin, flaking, redness, or sudden sensitivity means your routine is too much. Stop the exfoliant and simplify to gentle cleansing, moisturizer, and sunscreen until your baseline returns. Do not exfoliate harder to remove the flakes caused by exfoliating.

## 5. Protect Newly Exposed Skin

Exfoliation can make the surface more vulnerable to irritation, and some acids increase sun sensitivity. Apply broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher every morning and reapply during meaningful outdoor exposure.

## ⚠️ When to Ask a Dermatologist

Professional guidance is smart if you have eczema, rosacea, active dermatitis, prescription treatments, or recurring reactions. What looks like dullness or texture may be an inflammatory condition that exfoliation worsens.

## ✅ Bottom Line

Yes, some sensitive skin can be exfoliated—but “can” does not mean “must.” Begin with one gentle method, once weekly at most, and increase only if your skin remains comfortable. A calm barrier always outranks faster results.
