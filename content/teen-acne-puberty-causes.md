Acne is extremely common during the teenage years. Puberty hormones stimulate oil glands, and extra oil can combine with dead skin cells inside pores. When inflammation and *Cutibacterium acnes* bacteria join the process, blackheads, whiteheads and red pimples may appear.

**Teen acne is not proof of poor hygiene.** Scrubbing harder cannot wash out a clogged pore and often makes skin more irritated.

## 🧬 Why One Teen Gets More Acne Than Another

Hormones are only part of the story. Family history, skin type, medications, oily hair products, pore-clogging makeup and pressure from helmets or chin straps can affect breakouts. Stress may worsen existing acne, but it does not mean a student caused the condition by worrying.

Acne can affect the face, shoulders, chest and back. It may also look different across skin tones: redness is less obvious in deeper skin, while brown or gray marks can remain after a pimple settles.

## 🫧 Wash Gently, Not Constantly

Cleanse after waking, before bed and after heavy sweating. Use lukewarm water and fingertips with a mild, non-comedogenic cleanser. Skip grainy scrubs, cleansing brushes and alcohol-heavy astringents.

Washing five times daily does not control hormones. It can damage the barrier and make acne treatment harder to tolerate.

## 🎯 Treat the Type of Breakout

Salicylic acid helps unclog blackheads and whiteheads. Benzoyl peroxide can reduce acne-causing bacteria and inflamed pimples. Start one product at a low frequency and follow the label; using both everywhere on the first night invites dryness.

Apply treatment to the acne-prone area, not only today's visible spot, unless the label says it is a spot treatment. Give a consistent routine six to eight weeks before judging it, provided irritation stays mild.

## 💧 Moisturizer Does Not “Feed” Acne

Acne medication often dries skin. A lightweight, fragrance-free product labeled non-comedogenic can improve comfort and help a teen continue treatment. Apply it after medication, or before and after a retinoid if a clinician recommends buffering.

## 🚫 Do Not Pick or Punish the Skin

Squeezing pushes inflammation deeper and raises the chance of scars and dark marks. A hydrocolloid patch may protect a single surface pimple from fingers, but it cannot treat deep cysts.

See a clinician early for painful nodules, scarring, widespread chest or back acne, or distress about appearance. Effective prescription options exist, and seeking treatment is not vanity.

## ❓ Quick Questions

**Does chocolate cause every breakout?** No single food explains all acne. Some dietary patterns may affect some people, but skincare and appropriate treatment remain central.

**Will acne end at 18?** Not always. It may continue into the twenties or beyond.

**Is toothpaste a safe spot treatment?** No. It is formulated for teeth and can irritate skin.

## ✅ Bottom Line

Puberty creates conditions that make acne more likely; dirt and personal failure do not. Gentle cleansing, one suitable treatment, moisturizer and patience are a better starting plan than scrubbing or squeezing.
