If you've ever searched for a quick way to get rid of a pimple, you've probably come across one of the oldest skincare myths:

**"Put toothpaste on it."**

For years, people have claimed that toothpaste dries out pimples overnight.

But does it actually work?

Not really.

While toothpaste may temporarily dry the skin, it can also cause irritation, redness, and even make your skin barrier worse.

Here's what dermatologists recommend instead.

---

## 🦷 Why People Put Toothpaste on Pimples

The idea comes from older toothpaste formulas that contained drying ingredients.

People noticed that pimples sometimes looked smaller the next day.

However, modern toothpaste is designed for **teeth—not skin**.

Many formulas now contain ingredients that can irritate your face.

---

## 🚫 Why Toothpaste Isn't a Good Acne Treatment

Toothpaste may contain:

- Menthol
- Hydrogen peroxide
- Flavoring agents
- Whitening ingredients

These ingredients aren't made to treat acne and may cause:

- Dryness
- Redness
- Burning
- Skin irritation

Instead of healing your skin, they can damage your skin barrier.
