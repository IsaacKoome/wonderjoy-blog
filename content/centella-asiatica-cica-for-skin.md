Centella asiatica is a plant also called gotu kola. “Cica” products may contain its extract or isolated compounds such as madecassoside, asiaticoside, asiatic acid and madecassic acid. These ingredients are marketed for redness, sensitivity and barrier repair.

Laboratory and wound-care research is interesting, but a cosmetic cica cream should not be presented as treatment for an open wound or chronic skin disease.

## 🌿 What Research Suggests

Centella compounds have been studied for effects on inflammatory signaling, collagen production and re-epithelialization. A 2022 systematic review found only four clinical trials meeting its wound-healing criteria and concluded that more research was needed before firm meta-analysis.

A small cosmetic study of 25 volunteers found improved hydration and reduced experimentally induced redness with a 5% centella formulation after four weeks. Useful—but too small to promise that every “cica” product will calm rosacea or eczema.

## 🔍 “Cica” Does Not Describe One Formula

One product may use whole-plant extract; another may highlight madecassoside. Concentrations are often undisclosed. Fragrance, essential oils, alcohol and preservatives can matter more to reactive skin than the centella claim on the front.

Read the complete ingredient list. A simple fragrance-free moisturizer with centella is a safer first experiment than a ten-extract “calming” serum when your skin is already burning.

Texture matters too. A light centella gel may suit oily skin but provide too little protection for winter dryness. A rich cica balm may reduce flaking yet feel heavy on acne-prone areas. Choose the vehicle for your skin type instead of chasing the highest number of botanical extracts.

## 🧴 How to Add It

Patch-test for several days. Use a cica serum after cleansing and before moisturizer, or use a cica cream as the moisturizing step. Introduce only one new product so you can identify a reaction.

If skin is acutely irritated, pause exfoliants, strong vitamin C and retinoids. Cica should support a gentler routine, not help you tolerate excessive actives.

## ⚠️ Redness Has Many Causes

Temporary redness may follow over-exfoliation, heat or friction. Persistent flushing, visible vessels, scaling, itching or bumps may indicate rosacea, dermatitis or allergy. A botanical moisturizer cannot diagnose the difference.

Centella can also cause contact reactions in some people. Stop for worsening itch, swelling or a spreading rash. Do not apply cosmetic products to infected or open wounds unless directed by a clinician.

## ☀️ Protect Easily Flushed Skin

Sun and heat can aggravate redness and pigmentation. Use a comfortable broad-spectrum SPF 30+ and seek shade. A mineral sunscreen is often suggested for sting-prone skin, but formula tolerance remains individual.

## ❓ Quick Questions

**Is cica the same as tiger grass?** Yes, tiger grass is another marketing name for Centella asiatica.

**Can cica treat rosacea?** It may feel soothing, but it is not a proven replacement for rosacea diagnosis or medication.

**Can acne-prone skin use it?** Yes, if the complete product is lightweight and well tolerated. “Cica” alone does not guarantee non-comedogenicity.

## ✅ Bottom Line

Centella is a promising soothing and moisturizing ingredient, but cosmetic claims often run ahead of clinical evidence. Choose a simple formula and judge comfort—not hype. Give any new product several calm, consistent uses before deciding whether it helps, unless irritation tells you to stop sooner.
