Combination skin can feel confusing—you’re oily in some areas (like the T-zone) and dry in others (like the cheeks).

The key is balance. Instead of treating your whole face the same way, you need a routine that targets both concerns without overdoing it.

---

## 🤔 What Is Combination Skin?

Combination skin means:

- Oily forehead, nose, and chin (T-zone)  
- Dry or normal cheeks  
- Occasional breakouts + dry patches  

👉 This is one of the most common skin types.

## ⚖️ Pro Tips for Combination Skin

### ✔ Treat Different Areas Differently

- Use lightweight products on oily areas  
- Use richer creams on dry areas  

---

### ✔ Don’t Over-Cleanse

Washing too much can:
- Increase oil production  
- Dry out your skin  

👉 Stick to twice daily

---

### ✔ Use Targeted Treatments

- Clay masks → oily areas  
- Hydrating masks → dry areas  

---

## ❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Using harsh products that strip your skin  
- Skipping moisturizer  
- Using thick creams all over your face  
- Ignoring sunscreen  

---

## ⏳ When Will You See Results?

With consistency:

- Less oiliness in 1–2 weeks  
- Fewer dry patches in 2–3 weeks  
- Balanced skin in about a month  

---

## 🧠 Final Thoughts

Combination skin doesn’t need a complicated routine—it needs a smart one.

Focus on balance, use the right products, and treat different areas of your face accordingly.
