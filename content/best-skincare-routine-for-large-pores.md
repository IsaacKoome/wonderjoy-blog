Large pores are usually influenced by genetics, oil output, follicle size, congestion and skin firmness. No routine can permanently delete them, but the right steps can make them appear clearer and less prominent without creating irritation.

Here is a realistic routine—not a promise of poreless skin.

## 🌅 Morning Routine

### 1. Cleanse Gently

Use a mild cleanser and lukewarm water. If your skin is dry or sensitive, rinsing with water may be enough on some mornings. Oily skin may prefer a gentle cleanse. Avoid stiff brushes and gritty particles.

### 2. Use One Optional Oil-Supporting Serum

Niacinamide may help regulate the look of oil and support the barrier, but it is optional. A well-formulated moisturizer may already contain it. High percentages can sting, so more is not necessarily better.

### 3. Moisturize

Hydration can soften the appearance of surface roughness and helps skin tolerate acne treatments. Pick a texture you will actually use: lotion or gel-cream for oily skin, richer cream for dry skin. “Oil-free” is not a requirement for everyone, but acne-prone users can look for non-comedogenic labeling.

### 4. Apply Sunscreen

Use broad-spectrum, water-resistant SPF 30+ when outdoors. UV damage contributes to loss of firmness, which can make pores more noticeable. Sunscreen protects future texture rather than tightening today’s pores.

## 🌙 Evening Routine

### 1. Remove Sunscreen and Makeup

Cleanse carefully. A first cleansing step may help with heavy or water-resistant products, followed by a gentle water-based cleanser. Double cleansing is optional, not a competition.

### 2. Alternate Pore Treatments

Use salicylic acid two or three evenings weekly to address oil and dead cells inside follicles. On other nights, a retinoid may help recurrent blackheads and longer-term texture. Beginners should establish tolerance to one active before adding the other.

Do not combine a scrub, peel, BHA and retinoid in one ambitious night. Burning and flaking make pores look more noticeable and can trigger dark marks.

### 3. Finish With Moisturizer

Apply moisturizer after treatment. If a retinoid stings, try the sandwich method—moisturizer, retinoid, then another thin layer of moisturizer. Persistent burning is a reason to pause, not push through.

## 📅 A Beginner Schedule

- **Monday and Thursday:** salicylic acid
- **Tuesday and Saturday:** retinoid, if appropriate
- **Other nights:** cleanse and moisturize only

Adjust for tolerance. Sensitive skin may need one active night weekly. Ask a clinician about retinoids during pregnancy or breastfeeding.

## ⏳ When Will Pores Look Better?

Shine can change sooner, but congestion generally needs six to eight weeks. Texture and firmness take longer. Photograph your skin monthly in the same light and avoid judging it through a magnifying mirror.

If the routine works, maintain the lowest effective treatment frequency. Seasonal dryness, travel and hormonal changes may require adjustments. A routine is successful when it remains comfortable enough to follow, not when it contains the maximum possible number of actives.

## ❓ Quick Questions

**Should I exfoliate every day?** Usually not at first. Daily use can be too irritating.

**Can dry skin have large pores?** Yes. Follicle size, genetics and firmness matter beyond oiliness.

**When should I see a dermatologist?** For painful acne, scars, sudden major texture changes or no improvement after consistent care.

## ✅ Bottom Line

The best pore routine is boring and repeatable: gentle cleanse, controlled treatment, moisturizer and daily protection. Consistency improves appearance; no product removes pores.
