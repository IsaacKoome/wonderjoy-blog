Starting four exciting products on Sunday makes it almost impossible to identify the culprit when your face burns on Wednesday. A slower method may feel boring, but it gives you cleaner information and a better chance of keeping your skin comfortable.

## 1. Start With a Stable Baseline

Before adding anything, use a cleanser, moisturizer, and sunscreen that already feel comfortable. If your skin is actively peeling, swollen, sunburned, or rashy, wait. A new active cannot prove itself on skin that is already distressed.

## 2. Patch Test for 7–10 Days

Test a small area twice daily, following the product’s normal directions. Watch for redness, itching, bumps, swelling, blistering, or persistent stinging. A passed patch test lowers risk but does not guarantee facial tolerance.

## 3. Add Only One Product

Introduce the product to your face while every other step stays the same. For a basic cleanser or moisturizer, observe for at least one to two weeks. For an active aimed at long-term results, you may need longer to judge benefit—but irritation can appear much sooner.

## 4. Begin Actives Below Maximum Frequency

If directions allow, start a retinoid or exfoliant once or twice weekly rather than nightly. Do not begin two strong actives together. Increase only after your skin remains comfortable; faster use is not faster progress if it forces a recovery break.

## 5. Keep a Tiny Product Diary

Record the product, first-use date, frequency, and any symptoms. Photos in consistent lighting help distinguish a true pattern from a stressful morning. Note other changes such as weather, medication, shaving, or menstrual cycle.

## 🚨 What If You React?

Stop the newest product and return to your baseline. Rinse off anything that is still present. Seek medical help for a persistent or severe reaction and urgent care for facial swelling, widespread hives, breathing trouble, or severe blistering. Do not retry a suspected allergen alone.
