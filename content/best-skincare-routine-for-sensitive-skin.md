Sensitive skin usually does better with **fewer well-chosen products**, not a crowded shelf. The goal is comfortable, clean, moisturized, protected skin—not a tingling feeling that proves a product is “working.”

## 🌤️ Morning: Clean Only as Much as Needed

If you wake up dry and comfortable, rinsing with lukewarm water may be enough. If you are oily, sweaty, or used a heavy ointment overnight, massage a fragrance-free cleanser gently for about 30 seconds. Skip washcloth scrubbing and pat dry.

## 💧 Morning: Moisturize Before Skin Feels Tight

Apply a simple moisturizer while your skin is slightly damp. Look for familiar barrier-supporting ingredients such as glycerin, ceramides, or petrolatum. “Hypoallergenic” is not a guarantee, so the shortest ingredient list is not automatically the safest—your own history matters.

## ☀️ Morning: Finish With Sunscreen

Use broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher on exposed skin. Apply enough and reapply when outdoors, sweating, or after wiping your face. Mineral sunscreens containing zinc oxide or titanium dioxide may be comfortable for some reactive complexions, although texture and finish vary.

## 🌙 Evening: Remove the Day Gently

Cleanse once to remove sunscreen, makeup, and pollution. Heavy or water-resistant makeup may need a bland makeup remover first, followed by your gentle cleanser. Repeated washing can create more trouble than leftover “impurities.”

## 🌙 Evening: Moisturize and Stop

Use a comfortable cream rather than forcing an active serum into every night. If certain areas are very dry, a thin layer of petrolatum over moisturizer can reduce water loss. Avoid this over areas where it feels too occlusive or seems to worsen breakouts.

## 🧪 When Can You Add an Active?

Wait until your basic routine feels comfortable for at least two weeks. Patch test one product, begin once or twice weekly, and increase only if your skin stays calm. Burning, swelling, hives, or a persistent rash are reasons to stop—not to “push through.”
