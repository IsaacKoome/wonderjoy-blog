Horizontal neck lines can appear early, deepen with age, or simply reflect the way your neck folds when you move. Genetics, natural collagen changes, dryness, ultraviolet exposure, and repeated posture all contribute. Looking down at a phone may make existing folds more noticeable, but “tech neck” is not the only cause.

## ☀️ 1. Extend Sun Protection Below the Jaw

The neck and upper chest receive years of incidental UV exposure, yet many routines stop at the chin. Apply broad-spectrum SPF 30+ to the front, sides, and back of the neck when exposed. Add shade and protective clothing during long outdoor periods.

## 💧 2. Moisturize Consistently

Dryness makes lines look sharper. Apply moisturizer to slightly damp skin after washing, using gentle upward or downward strokes without aggressive rubbing. A richer cream may feel better on the neck than a lightweight facial gel.

## 🌙 3. Introduce Retinol Carefully

Retinol may modestly improve fine lines and uneven texture, but neck skin often irritates more easily than facial skin. Mix or layer a small amount with moisturizer once weekly, then increase only if there is no burning or peeling. Do not use retinoids during pregnancy.

## 📱 4. Change Habits for Comfort, Not Perfection

Raise screens closer to eye level, take movement breaks, and avoid holding the neck in one position for hours. This can help posture and discomfort, though it will not erase established lines. Natural folds still appear whenever the neck bends.

![Person applying sunscreen and moisturizer to the neck correctly](/images/neck-skincare-sunscreen-moisturizer.png)

## 🚫 Skip Harsh Shortcuts

Strong peels, scrubs, and fragranced oils can trigger irritation or dark marks. Do not copy a strong facial routine directly onto the neck. Stop products that sting and simplify until the skin recovers.

## 🩺 When Creams Cannot Do Enough

Topicals hydrate and may modestly improve fine texture; they cannot fully tighten loose skin or remove deep folds. A board-certified dermatologist can explain procedures such as prescription retinoids, resurfacing, injectables, or tightening treatments and their risks.

## ⏳ What Timeline Is Realistic?

Moisturizer can soften dryness quickly, while any change from retinol takes months. Compare photos no more than monthly, with your head held in the same position and the lighting unchanged. Neck movement can create or hide folds from one picture to the next, so dramatic overnight “results” usually say more about pose and light than treatment.

Treat the neck as part of your routine: protect, moisturize, and introduce actives more slowly than you would on the face. Improvement is gradual, and some neck lines are a normal part of movement and aging.
