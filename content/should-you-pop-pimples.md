A pimple with a visible white center can feel impossible to ignore. Popping it may create a few seconds of satisfaction, but it can also turn a small blemish into a longer-lasting problem.

Dermatologists generally recommend **not popping pimples at home**. Pressure can push some of the contents deeper into the skin, increasing inflammation, pain, infection risk, discoloration, and scarring.

---

## Pimples You Should Never Squeeze

Keep your hands off:

- Deep, painful cysts or nodules
- Red bumps without a visible head
- Blemishes near the eyes
- Spots that are hot, rapidly swelling, or crusting

Deep acne is especially likely to scar and usually cannot be emptied safely by squeezing the surface.

## What to Do Instead

### For a Deep, Painful Pimple

Apply a warm, damp washcloth for 10 to 15 minutes a few times during the day. Ice wrapped in a clean cloth can briefly reduce discomfort. Do not place ice directly on the skin.

### For an Inflamed Surface Pimple

Try a small amount of benzoyl peroxide. It targets acne-causing bacteria and inflammation, but can be drying and may bleach fabric.

### For a Whitehead That Has Opened Naturally

Clean the area gently and cover it with a hydrocolloid patch. The patch can absorb fluid, protect the spot, and stop absent-minded picking.

![Protected healing pimple under a hydrocolloid patch](/images/pimple-healing-with-hydrocolloid-patch.png)

## If You Already Popped It

1. Stop squeezing.
2. Wash your hands and cleanse the area gently.
3. Do not apply toothpaste, alcohol, lemon juice, or harsh scrubs.
4. Cover the clean, dry spot with a hydrocolloid patch.
5. Use sunscreen after the skin closes to reduce lingering discoloration.

Watch for spreading redness, worsening pain, warmth, or pus. Those signs deserve medical advice.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are blackhead tools safe at home?

Metal extractors can bruise skin, break capillaries, spread bacteria, and create scars when used with too much pressure. A leave-on salicylic acid or retinoid is slower but safer for repeated blackheads. Professional extraction is the better option for stubborn plugs.

### Can a pimple patch pull out a closed pimple?

Hydrocolloid works best on a surface pimple that is open or releasing fluid. It cannot reach a deep cyst or magically pull a closed comedone through intact skin. Its biggest benefit may be protecting the spot from fingers.

### How do dermatologists flatten a painful cyst quickly?

A dermatologist may inject an appropriate corticosteroid into certain large, painful blemishes. This is a medical procedure; attempting to inject, lance, or drain a cyst at home can cause infection and permanent scarring.

## Final Thoughts

The safest time to extract a pimple is during a professional procedure performed with sterile tools and the right technique. At home, protect the spot and treat the acne instead of forcing it out.
