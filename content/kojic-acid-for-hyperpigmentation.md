Kojic acid is produced from certain fungi and fermentation processes. In skincare, it is used to reduce the appearance of excess pigment—not to bleach every part of your natural skin tone. It can be useful for post-acne marks, sun spots and melasma, but the ingredient is also known for irritation and stability problems.

That makes “start slowly” more than generic advice.

## 🔬 How Kojic Acid Works

Melanin production relies on an enzyme called tyrosinase. Kojic acid can bind at the enzyme’s active site and reduce its activity, which may slow the formation of new excess pigment. It does not physically scrub pigment from the surface.

A 2025 randomized pilot study compared a cream containing 5% alpha arbutin plus 2% kojic acid with prescription triple-combination cream for melasma. Results were encouraging, but only 30 participants were included and the kojic acid was not tested alone. Realistic claims must reflect that limitation.

## 🎯 Which Dark Marks May Respond?

Kojic acid is most relevant to **flat discoloration**: brown post-acne marks, uneven tone, melasma and some sun-related spots. It cannot repair indented or raised scars. New, changing, bleeding or irregular pigmented lesions require a clinician before any brightening treatment.

Visible improvement generally takes weeks. If the inflammation causing pigment continues—such as uncontrolled acne or eczema—new marks can replace the old ones.

## 🧴 How to Introduce It

Patch-test for several days. Start two or three evenings per week after cleansing, followed by moisturizer. Increase only if your skin remains comfortable and the product label allows it.

Avoid beginning kojic acid in the same week as a retinoid, strong exfoliant or benzoyl peroxide. Once tolerance is clear, alternating nights is often simpler than stacking everything together. More burning does not mean faster fading.

## ⚠️ Irritation Can Darken Marks

Kojic acid may cause redness, itching, dryness or allergic contact dermatitis. Irritation is especially important for deeper skin tones because inflammation can create additional hyperpigmentation. Stop if burning persists or a rash spreads.

Products can also discolor or lose stability with heat, air and light. Follow storage directions and do not assume a darkened or old formula remains effective.

## ☀️ Daily Protection Is Non-Negotiable

UV and visible light can reactivate pigmentation. Use broad-spectrum SPF 30+ every morning, reapply outdoors, and add shade or a hat. A tinted sunscreen with iron oxides may be helpful for melasma and persistent pigment.

## ❓ Quick Questions

**Can kojic acid lighten normal skin?** Apply it only as directed for uneven pigment. Diffuse lightening and irritation are reasons to stop and seek advice.

**Can I use it with vitamin C?** Some routines do, but start separately. Combination formulas should be judged as one product.

**Is kojic soap better than serum?** Cleansers have short contact time; soaps may also be drying. A leave-on product allows controlled use but can still irritate.

## ✅ Bottom Line

Kojic acid may help flat hyperpigmentation by reducing tyrosinase activity. Begin slowly, moisturize, protect against sunlight and stop before irritation creates a bigger pigment problem. Photograph the area monthly in similar lighting; daily mirror checks can make gradual, uneven progress difficult to recognize. A steady, tolerable routine usually beats a stronger routine you cannot maintain.
