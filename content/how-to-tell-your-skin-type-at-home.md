Knowing your skin type is one of the most important steps in building an effective skincare routine.

If you've ever wondered why a moisturizer works for your friend but causes breakouts on your skin, your skin type is probably the reason.

The good news?

You don't need expensive equipment or a dermatologist to get started. A simple test at home can give you a good idea of your skin type.

Let's find out.

---

## 🧴 The Simple 30-Minute Skin Type Test

Follow these steps:

1. Wash your face with a gentle cleanser.
2. Pat your skin dry with a clean towel.
3. Don't apply any skincare products.
4. Wait about **30 minutes**.

Now take a close look in the mirror.

---

## 🔍 What Does Your Skin Feel Like?

### 🟢 Oily Skin

If your forehead, nose, and cheeks look shiny or greasy, you probably have oily skin.

Signs include:

- Visible shine
- Enlarged pores
- Frequent acne or blackheads

---

### 🟤 Dry Skin

If your face feels tight or rough after washing, your skin may be dry.

Common signs:

- Flaky patches
- Tight feeling
- Dull appearance
- Fine lines become more noticeable

---

### 🟡 Combination Skin

Combination skin is the most common skin type.

You may notice:

- Oily forehead, nose, and chin (T-zone)
- Dry or normal cheeks
- Occasional breakouts

---

### 🔵 Normal Skin

Normal skin feels comfortable throughout the day.

Signs include:

- Little excess oil
- Few dry patches
- Small pores
- Smooth texture

---

### 🟣 Sensitive Skin

Sensitive skin isn't really a skin type, but many people experience it.

You may notice:

- Burning or stinging
- Redness
- Easy irritation
- Reactions to new products

## ⚖️ Quick Skin Type Guide

| Skin Type | Main Characteristics |
|------------|----------------------|
| Oily | Shiny skin, enlarged pores, acne-prone |
| Dry | Tight, flaky, rough texture |
| Combination | Oily T-zone with drier cheeks |
| Normal | Balanced oil and moisture |
| Sensitive | Easily irritated or becomes red |

---

## 💡 Tips for Every Skin Type

- Wash your face twice daily.
- Moisturize every day.
- Wear sunscreen every morning.
- Introduce new products gradually.
- Avoid harsh scrubs that can damage your skin barrier.

---

## ✨ Final Thoughts

Understanding your skin type makes choosing skincare products much easier.

Whether your skin is oily, dry, combination, normal, or sensitive, using products designed for your needs can help improve your skin's appearance and keep it healthy.

If your skin changes throughout the year, don't worry—that's completely normal. Hormones, weather, and age can all affect your skin over time.
