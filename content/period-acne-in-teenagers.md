Many teenagers notice more pimples in the days before a menstrual period. Hormone shifts can increase oil production and inflammation, often producing breakouts on the chin, jawline or lower cheeks. The pattern is common, but it is not something a teen must silently endure.

**The best plan begins before the flare.** Consistent prevention is more useful than attacking each monthly breakout with several strong products.

Record the first day of each period and take one weekly skin photo in similar light. Note painful bumps, new products and stress. A repeatable premenstrual pattern becomes easier to discuss with a pediatrician, dermatologist or gynecology professional.

Do not diagnose a hormonal disorder from acne alone. Teen cycles can be irregular early after the first period, and PCOS assessment requires clinical context.
