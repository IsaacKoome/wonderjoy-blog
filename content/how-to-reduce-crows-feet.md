Crow’s feet are the small lines that fan from the outer corners of the eyes. They form partly because smiling and squinting repeatedly fold the skin, and they become more visible as skin naturally loses moisture, elasticity, and collagen. They are normal—not evidence that you did skincare “wrong.”

## 🕶️ 1. Reduce Preventable Squinting

UV exposure accelerates premature aging, while bright light encourages repeated squinting. Wear UV-protective sunglasses, seek shade, and use broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher every day. Choose a sunscreen that does not sting your eyes and apply it gently around the orbital bone.

## 💧 2. Hydrate the Surface

Dryness exaggerates small creases. A fragrance-free moisturizer can temporarily plump the outer layer and make fine lines look softer. Tap on a small amount instead of pulling the skin. Expensive eye cream is optional if your facial moisturizer feels comfortable.

## 🌙 3. Consider Retinol Carefully

Retinol may modestly improve mild fine lines with consistent use, but the eye area irritates easily. Use a product labeled for facial or eye-area use, begin once or twice weekly, and keep it away from eyelids and the lash line. A rice-grain amount for both outer eye areas is plenty.

## 🧴 4. Cushion Sensitive Skin

Apply moisturizer before retinol if you are dryness-prone, then add another thin layer afterward. Stop for burning, swelling, persistent peeling, or rash. Avoid retinoids during pregnancy and ask a dermatologist before using them if you have eczema or rosacea near the eyes.

## 🚫 What Not to Do

- Do not scrub lines with exfoliating brushes.
- Do not apply strong acids close to the eye.
- Do not assume peeling means retinol is working better.
- Do not expect water, patches, or one cream to erase movement lines.

## 🩺 When Skincare Is Not Enough

Topicals create modest changes. A dermatologist can discuss procedures for deeper or movement-related lines, including their risks, costs, and realistic results. Seek care promptly for sudden swelling, vision symptoms, or a painful eye-area rash.

## 📸 Track Comfort, Not Perfection

Take one neutral-light photo each month with the same expression. Daily close-up checks exaggerate normal texture and encourage over-treatment. A useful result may be softer dryness and comfortable skin rather than erased lines. Remember that smiling will always fold the outer eye—and that movement is exactly what healthy faces are designed to do.
