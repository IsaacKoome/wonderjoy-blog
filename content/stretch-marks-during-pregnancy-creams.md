Stretch marks are a type of scar that develops when skin stretches or shrinks quickly. During pregnancy they commonly appear on the abdomen, breasts, hips, thighs or buttocks. Genetics, hormonal influences and the speed of body change all matter—this is not evidence that someone failed to moisturize.

ACOG states there is no proof that marketed treatments prevent stretch marks. A heavy moisturizer can keep skin comfortable, but it cannot guarantee prevention or erase the deeper structural change.

## 🔍 What Early and Older Marks Look Like

New stretch marks may look red, pink, purple, reddish-brown or dark brown depending on skin tone. Over time they often become lighter and less noticeable. They may itch while developing but should not form blisters, open wounds or a widespread inflamed rash.

A pregnancy rash that is intensely itchy, rapidly spreading or blistering needs maternity care rather than stretch-mark oil.

## 💧 Moisturizer Helps Comfort—not Prevention

Apply a fragrance-free cream or lotion after bathing when skin is still slightly damp. This can ease tightness and surface dryness. Massage gently if comfortable, but stop if the skin becomes sore or irritated.

## 🧾 Be Skeptical of “Clinically Proven” Oils

Cocoa butter, shea butter, vitamin E and botanical oils can make skin feel softer, but softness is not proof of scar prevention. Fragrance and essential oils can also trigger contact dermatitis. Check whether a claim describes hydration, appearance or actual prevention—and who funded the study.

A thicker fragrance-free cream may last longer on very dry skin. Choose it for comfort rather than a promise of permanent removal.

## ⛔ Avoid Retinoids During Pregnancy

The AAD notes that tretinoin or retinol may improve some early stretch marks, but retinoids should not be used during pregnancy. Do not buy a scar cream without reading the active ingredients. Discuss postpartum treatment timing if breastfeeding.

Procedures such as lasers, microneedling or other resurfacing treatments cannot promise complete removal and should wait for evaluation by a qualified dermatologist.

## ☀️ Protect Exposed Marks

Sun protection will not remove stretch marks, but it reduces tanning contrast and protects the surrounding skin. Cover exposed areas or use broad-spectrum SPF 30+.

## ❓ Quick Questions

**Does scratching create stretch marks?** No. Scratching may injure irritated surface skin, but stretch marks form deeper as skin changes rapidly.

**Can weight control guarantee prevention?** No. Follow healthy pregnancy weight guidance from your maternity team, not restrictive advice marketed around stretch marks.

**When should treatment be discussed?** After delivery, once marks and feeding plans can be assessed. Earlier marks may respond differently from mature marks, but complete removal is unlikely.

## ✅ Bottom Line

No cream can guarantee that pregnancy stretch marks will not form. Moisturize for comfort, avoid irritating miracle products, protect exposed skin and remember that marks often fade naturally after delivery.
