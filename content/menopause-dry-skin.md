Dryness is one of the most common menopause skin complaints. Skin may feel tight after washing, look dull or flaky, and crack more easily on the hands, legs and heels. Hormonal change contributes, while normal aging also reduces oil and sweat production and changes the skin's structure.

**You cannot moisturize hormone levels back to where they were, but you can reduce water loss and irritation.**

## 🚿 Shorten the Hot Shower

Use warm rather than hot water and keep showers brief. Clean the face, underarms, groin, feet and visibly dirty areas; the whole body does not always need strong soap. Choose mild fragrance-free cleanser and apply it with your hands instead of a rough sponge.

Pat until skin is damp. Moisturizer works best when it can trap some of that water.

## 🧴 Choose Cream or Ointment Over Thin Lotion

Creams and ointments usually reduce water loss better than runny lotions. Look for glycerin, ceramides, hyaluronic acid or petrolatum, and avoid fragrance when skin is itchy or reactive. Apply within a few minutes of bathing and again whenever hands or other areas feel dry.

For cracked hands or heels, use a thick layer at bedtime and soft cotton gloves or socks if comfortable. Do not seal over hot, painful, oozing or infected skin.

## 🏠 Reduce Hidden Drying Factors

Indoor heating, air conditioning, low humidity and frequent handwashing can all add to dryness. A clean humidifier may help in a dry bedroom. Wear gloves for wet work, switch from fragranced detergent and fabric softener if they irritate you, and choose soft clothing next to the skin.

Drinking enough fluid supports general health, but extra water does not replace a damaged surface barrier. Treat the outside of the skin directly.

## ☀️ Protect Fragile, Exposed Skin

Apply broad-spectrum, water-resistant SPF 30 or higher to exposed areas. Add a hat, shade and protective clothing. Sunscreen does not moisturize every formula equally, so layer it over moisturizer if the label allows and your skin feels comfortable.

## 🚩 When Dryness Needs a Diagnosis

Persistent itch may come from eczema, medication, thyroid disease, diabetes, kidney or liver problems—not menopause alone. Seek care for widespread itch without a rash, bleeding cracks, signs of infection, sleep-disrupting symptoms or dryness that does not improve with gentle care.

## ❓ Quick Questions

**Are body oils enough?** Oil can reduce water loss, but it does not add water. Many people do better applying cream first and oil only if tolerated.

**Should I scrub off flakes?** No. Scrubbing can create more inflammation. Soften with moisturizer and let loose scale lift gently.

**Is hyaluronic acid always hydrating?** It is a useful humectant, but needs a moisturizer on top in dry conditions. Stop if a formula stings.

## ✅ Bottom Line

Warm short washing, immediate rich moisturizer, fewer irritants and daily sun protection are the practical core of menopause dry-skin care. Severe or stubborn itching deserves medical evaluation.
