Yes, acne can appear or return around menopause. Hormonal fluctuations can contribute, but blocked pores, products, medication and friction may also play a role. The awkward part is that mature skin can be breakout-prone and dry at the same time.

**Treat the acne you have now, not the oily teenage skin you remember.** Harsh scrubs and drying astringents often trade pimples for irritation.

## 🫧 Cleanse Gently Twice—or Once—Daily

Use a mild non-comedogenic cleanser at night and after sweating. Cleanse in the morning only if needed. Lukewarm water and fingertips are enough; brushes and grainy scrubs do not unclog pores more effectively.

## 🎯 Pick One Acne Active

Salicylic acid can help unclog blackheads and small bumps. Benzoyl peroxide reduces acne-causing bacteria and may suit inflamed pimples, but it can dry skin and bleach fabric. Azelaic acid can help mild acne and post-breakout discoloration and is often considered when sensitivity is a concern.

Start one product two or three times weekly, following its label. Apply only the recommended amount and increase slowly. Do not begin salicylic acid, benzoyl peroxide and a retinoid on the same night.

## 💧 Moisturizer Is Part of Acne Treatment

A non-comedogenic, fragrance-free moisturizer can reduce tightness and make active treatment easier to continue. Apply it after treatment, or use the “sandwich” approach—moisturizer, retinoid, moisturizer—if a clinician recommends a retinoid and your skin is easily irritated.

If the face burns or flakes continuously, stop the active and rebuild comfort with cleanser, moisturizer and sunscreen.

## ☀️ Prevent Marks From Getting Darker

Use broad-spectrum, water-resistant SPF 30 or higher every morning. This matters particularly if acne leaves brown marks or if your treatment increases sun sensitivity. Do not pick: squeezing raises the risk of prolonged marks and scars.

## 🚩 Get Help for Painful or Hormonal Patterns

See a dermatologist for deep painful nodules, scarring, sudden severe acne, or breakouts with rapidly increasing facial hair or scalp hair loss. Prescription topical treatments, spironolactone or other options may be appropriate after a clinician reviews your health and medicines. Never take someone else's hormonal medication.

## ❓ Quick Questions

**Is jawline acne always hormonal?** No. Location can offer clues, but it cannot diagnose the cause.

**Will sun dry out acne?** A tan can temporarily mask redness, but UV damages skin and can worsen discoloration.

**How long should treatment take?** Most acne routines need six to eight weeks for a fair assessment, provided irritation is controlled.

## ✅ Bottom Line

Menopause acne needs a balanced approach: gentle cleansing, one suitable active, daily moisturizer and sun protection. Escalate with a dermatologist rather than making the routine harsher.
