A clean face should not feel as if it is on fire. Brief discomfort can have several causes, but repeated **stinging after washing** is a signal to examine your cleanser, technique, and entire routine.

## 1. Your Cleanser Is Too Harsh

Strong detergents, deodorant soaps, or highly fragranced washes can remove too much oil and irritate reactive skin. A squeaky finish is not the goal; comfortable skin is.

## 2. The Water Is Too Hot

Hot water can worsen dryness and make an already vulnerable barrier feel tight. Use lukewarm water and keep washing brief.

## 3. You Are Washing Too Often

Cleansing after waking, exercising, arriving home, and before bed may be too much. Most people need a thorough evening cleanse; your morning needs depend on oil, sweat, and overnight products.

## 4. Your Barrier Is Already Irritated

Retinoids, exfoliating acids, acne treatments, scrubs, shaving, dry weather, or sunburn may prime skin to sting when water touches it. The cleanser may expose the problem rather than be the only cause.

## 5. You Are Scrubbing or Rubbing

Brushes, rough cloths, grainy scrubs, and vigorous towel-drying add friction. Use clean fingertips, rinse thoroughly, then pat—not rub—with a soft towel.

## 6. You May Be Reacting to an Ingredient

Fragrance, preservatives, botanical extracts, or another ingredient can trigger irritant or allergic contact dermatitis. “Natural” and “for sensitive skin” do not rule out a reaction. Stop the new suspect and seek care if a rash persists.

## 7. It May Be a Skin Condition

Eczema, rosacea, and other conditions can cause burning, redness, scaling, or itching. Get medical help promptly for facial swelling, hives, blisters, oozing, eye symptoms, severe pain, or breathing difficulty.

## 🧯 What to Do Tonight

Rinse gently with lukewarm water, apply a fragrance-free moisturizer, and pause acids, scrubs, retinoids, and strong acne treatments. During the day, protect exposed skin from sun. Reintroduce products one at a time only after comfort returns.

## ✅ Bottom Line

Stinging is information, not proof that cleansing is effective. Reduce heat, friction, frequency, and formula complexity. If the sensation keeps returning despite a simple routine, a dermatologist can help identify the cause.
