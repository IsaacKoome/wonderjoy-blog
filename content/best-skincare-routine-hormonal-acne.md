Hormonal acne often appears as tender, deep breakouts around the chin and jawline. It may flare before a period, after changing birth control, during pregnancy, or around perimenopause.

Skincare can reduce clogged pores and inflammation, but it cannot always control the hormonal trigger. The best routine is simple enough to follow consistently and gentle enough to avoid adding irritation.

---

## Morning Routine

### 1. Cleanse Gently

Use lukewarm water and a fragrance-free cleanser. Scrubbing harder will not remove hormonal acne and may make redness worse.

### 2. Choose One Acne Active

Salicylic acid can help unclog pores. Benzoyl peroxide can be useful for inflamed pimples. Start with one, two or three mornings per week, then increase only if your skin stays comfortable.

### 3. Moisturize

Choose a lightweight product labeled non-comedogenic or “won't clog pores.” Moisturizer can improve tolerance to acne treatments.

### 4. Apply Sunscreen

Use broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher every day. This helps stop post-acne marks from becoming darker and more persistent.

---

## Night Routine

1. Remove makeup and cleanse gently.
2. Apply your dermatologist-recommended treatment or a suitable over-the-counter acne active.
3. Finish with moisturizer.

Adapalene is an evidence-based retinoid for blackheads, whiteheads, and pimples, but retinoids can irritate and are not appropriate during pregnancy. Ask a clinician if you are pregnant, trying to conceive, or unsure what to use.

## Mistakes That Slow Progress

- Starting several strong products at once
- Using harsh scrubs on painful bumps
- Treating only visible pimples instead of the acne-prone area
- Switching routines every week
- Picking deep jawline acne

Topical treatments commonly need six to eight weeks for early improvement and longer for fuller results.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Should I use salicylic acid and benzoyl peroxide together?

Some routines eventually include both, but starting them together makes dryness and irritation more likely. Begin with one active, assess your skin for several weeks, then ask a dermatologist whether alternating products would add value.

### Does hormonal acne ever respond to skincare alone?

Mild breakouts can improve with topical treatment, especially when clogged pores and inflammation are controlled. Recurring deep jawline acne may need prescription hormonal or other medical treatment because topical products do not change the underlying hormone signal.

### What if my acne flares before every period?

Track the timing for two or three cycles and bring the record to a medical appointment. A predictable pattern can help a clinician assess hormonal acne and discuss options appropriate for your health history.

## When to See a Dermatologist

Get help if breakouts are deep, painful, scarring, suddenly severe, or accompanied by irregular periods or new facial hair. Prescription options can address hormonal drivers that skincare alone cannot.
