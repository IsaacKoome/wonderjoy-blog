## ❌ Myth 1: You Need a 10-Step Skincare Routine

Many people believe more products = better skin.

👉 **Truth:** A simple routine works best.

- Cleanser  
- Moisturizer  
- Sunscreen  

That’s enough for most people.

---

## ❌ Myth 2: Oily Skin Doesn’t Need Moisturizer

This is one of the biggest mistakes beginners make.

👉 **Truth:** Skipping moisturizer can make your skin produce *more* oil.

Use a lightweight, non-greasy moisturizer instead.

## ❌ Myth 3: Natural Products Are Always Better

Natural doesn’t always mean effective—or safe.

👉 **Truth:** Some natural ingredients can irritate your skin, while many synthetic ingredients are safe and proven.

Always focus on ingredients, not labels.

---

## ❌ Myth 4: You Don’t Need Sunscreen Indoors

Many people skip sunscreen if they’re not going outside.

👉 **Truth:** UV rays can still reach your skin through windows.

Daily sunscreen is essential—even indoors.

## ❌ Myth 5: Washing Your Face More Prevents Acne

It might seem logical—but it actually backfires.

👉 **Truth:** Overwashing strips your skin and can cause more breakouts.

Stick to washing your face **twice daily**.

---

## ❌ Myth 6: Expensive Products Work Better

High price doesn’t always mean high quality.

👉 **Truth:** Many affordable products work just as well—if not better.

---

## ❌ Myth 7: You Should Feel a “Tingling” Sensation

Some people think tingling = product working.

👉 **Truth:** Tingling often means irritation.

Skincare should feel gentle, not painful.

---

## ❌ Myth 8: You Can Shrink Your Pores

This is a very common belief.

👉 **Truth:** You can’t permanently shrink pores—but you can make them look smaller by keeping them clean.

Use a gentle cleanser regularly.

## ❌ Myth 9: Skincare Works Instantly

People expect results in a few days.

👉 **Truth:** Real results take time—usually 4–8 weeks.

Consistency is key.

---

## ❌ Myth 10: Drinking Water Alone Clears Skin

Water is important—but it’s not a miracle cure.

👉 **Truth:** Good skincare + healthy habits = best results.

---

## 🧠 Final Thoughts

Skincare doesn’t have to be confusing. Most myths exist because of misinformation or marketing hype.

Stick to simple, proven basics and focus on consistency—not trends.
