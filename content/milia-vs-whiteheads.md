Tiny white bumps are easy to label as “clogged pores,” but **milia and whiteheads are different**. Whiteheads are a mild form of acne. Milia are small cysts filled with trapped keratin beneath the surface.

The safest response is not to squeeze first and identify later.

## 🔍 How They Usually Look

**Milia** tend to be firm, pearly-white or yellowish bumps. They often appear around the eyelids, under the eyes or on the upper cheeks. They are usually painless and may persist for weeks or longer.

**Whiteheads** are closed comedones. They form when oil and dead cells block a follicle opening. They commonly appear on the forehead, nose, chin, chest or back and may occur beside blackheads and pimples.

Location offers a clue, not a diagnosis. A dermatologist can distinguish milia from acne, sebaceous hyperplasia and other bumps.

## 🫧 Use a Gentle Base Routine

Wash up to twice daily with lukewarm water and a mild cleanser. Do not scrub the bump or try to sand it flat. Around the eyes, avoid strong acne cleansers unless the label specifically allows use there.

## 🧪 Treating Whiteheads

Salicylic acid can help unclog follicles, while topical retinoids help clear and prevent comedones. Begin slowly and apply across the acne-prone area, avoiding eyelids and irritated skin. Benzoyl peroxide is more useful when inflammatory pimples accompany the whiteheads.

Give treatment six to eight weeks. Constantly switching products makes it hard to know what works and often creates irritation that resembles new bumps.

## 👩‍⚕️ Treating Milia

Milia are harmless and sometimes resolve without treatment. An over-the-counter adapalene or prescription tretinoin may be suggested for selected adult cases, but applying a retinoid close to the eye without professional guidance is risky.

If a persistent milium bothers you, a healthcare professional can open and remove it using sterile technique. Do not copy the procedure with a sewing needle, lancet or extractor. The cyst sits under a layer of skin, so forceful squeezing may cause a wound, infection, scar or dark mark without removing it.

## 💧 Do Heavy Creams Cause Milia?

People often blame eye cream, but milia can develop without any heavy product. Occlusive formulas may feel unsuitable for some users, yet one ingredient cannot explain every bump. Stop a suspected new product and observe rather than stripping the area with acids.

Use a comfortable moisturizer on the rest of the face. A damaged, flaky barrier does not help either condition.

## 🚩 When to Seek Help

Arrange an examination if bumps rapidly multiply, itch, hurt, crust, affect the eye or do not resemble your usual acne. Persistent white bumps can have several causes, and treating the wrong one for months wastes time.

Before the appointment, note when the bumps appeared, whether they change and which products touch the area. Clear photographs can help show progression, especially if the texture varies from week to week.

## ❓ Quick Questions

**Are milia acne?** No. They are keratin-filled cysts.

**Can a whitehead become a pimple?** Yes, inflammation can develop in a clogged follicle.

**Can I pop either one?** No. Home squeezing increases injury and infection risk.

## ✅ Bottom Line

Firm, persistent bumps near the eyes suggest milia; acne-prone clusters with blackheads suggest whiteheads. When uncertain, protect the skin and get the bump identified before treating aggressively.
