The acne aisle can make a teenager feel as though five active ingredients are required immediately. They are not. Salicylic acid, benzoyl peroxide and adapalene work differently, and the best starting choice depends on the type of breakout and skin tolerance.

**Choose one main treatment, use it correctly and give it time.** Painful or scarring acne should skip the trial-and-error stage and be assessed by a clinician.

## ⚪ Salicylic Acid for Clogged Pores

Salicylic acid helps loosen material inside pores. It is most useful for blackheads, whiteheads and small rough bumps. A cleanser may be easier to tolerate than a leave-on liquid because it is rinsed away.

Begin every other day or a few times weekly. Daily use plus a scrub, toner and peeling mask can quickly create burning and flakes.

## 🔴 Benzoyl Peroxide for Inflamed Pimples

Benzoyl peroxide targets acne-causing bacteria and can help red, pus-filled pimples. Lower strengths may work with less irritation than maximum-strength products. It can bleach towels, pillowcases and clothing, so rinse washes thoroughly and use white fabrics when practical.

Follow the product age directions and avoid eyes, lips and broken skin. Stop and seek help for swelling, blistering or breathing difficulty.

## 🌙 Adapalene for Prevention

Adapalene is a retinoid that helps keep pores from clogging. When appropriate for the teen's age and health, it is applied as a thin layer over the acne-prone area—not as a large dab on one pimple. A pea-sized amount can cover the face.

Start two or three nights weekly, moisturize and increase gradually. It can cause dryness and sun sensitivity. Follow the label and ask a healthcare professional about retinoid use during pregnancy or when pregnancy is possible.

## 💧 Protect the Barrier Around Treatment

Use a gentle cleanser, lightweight non-comedogenic moisturizer and broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher. Add no other active until the first is comfortable. Mild dryness can happen; persistent burning, swelling or cracking means pause and get advice.

## 🗓️ Judge Progress Monthly, Not Daily

Fewer new breakouts may take six to eight weeks, and fuller improvement often takes longer. Take one photo monthly in similar light. Constantly changing products resets the experiment and increases irritation.

Do not use a friend's prescription, oral antibiotic or isotretinoin. Prescription treatment requires the right diagnosis, monitoring and pregnancy precautions where relevant.

## ❓ Quick Questions

**Can all three be used together?** Some clinician-designed routines combine them, but beginners should not create an aggressive combination alone.

**What if acne gets worse at first?** Mild adjustment can occur with retinoids, but severe inflammation or rash is not something to push through.

**Are pimple patches a treatment plan?** They may protect individual surface pimples from picking but do not prevent widespread acne.

## ✅ Bottom Line

Use salicylic acid mainly for clogged pores, benzoyl peroxide for inflamed pimples and adapalene for ongoing prevention when appropriate. Begin slowly and support every option with moisturizer and sunscreen.
