Your 30s do not require a shelf full of “anti-aging” products. They require a routine you can repeat. Early fine lines, lingering dark marks, dryness, and adult acne often respond best to the same foundation: gentle cleansing, targeted treatment, moisturizer, and daily sun protection.

## ☀️ Morning: Protect What You Have

### 1. Cleanse only as needed

Use a gentle cleanser if you wake oily. Dry or sensitive skin may prefer a water rinse. Avoid scrubbing; irritation makes texture and discoloration more noticeable.

### 2. Add one antioxidant

Vitamin C can support brighter, more even-looking skin and complement sunscreen. Apply a few drops before moisturizer. If it stings, use it less often or skip it—no single serum is essential.

Choose a light lotion for oily skin or a cream for dryness. Moisturizer traps water, supports the barrier, and can temporarily soften dehydration lines.

### 4. Finish with sunscreen

Use broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher on the face, ears, neck, and exposed chest. Reapply during prolonged outdoor exposure. This step prevents more premature aging than any luxury cream.

## 🌙 Night: Repair Without Overloading

Cleanse away makeup, sweat, and sunscreen. On two nights weekly, apply a pea-sized amount of low-strength retinol to dry skin, followed by moisturizer. On the other nights, moisturize only. Increase slowly if your skin remains calm.

## 📅 A Simple Weekly Schedule

- **Monday and Thursday:** retinol nights
- **Other nights:** cleanse and moisturize
- **Every morning:** sunscreen
- **If irritated:** pause actives until skin feels normal

Pregnant people should avoid retinoids. Persistent acne, sudden pigment changes, or painful rashes deserve a dermatologist’s assessment.

## 🧩 Adjust the Routine, Not the Goal

If acne is your main concern, use a proven acne treatment instead of adding several anti-aging serums. If dryness is the problem, reduce active nights and choose a richer moisturizer. Sensitive skin may do best with only cleanser, moisturizer, and sunscreen for a month before adding anything else. Seasonal changes also matter: a gel that feels perfect in humid weather may not be enough during a dry winter.
