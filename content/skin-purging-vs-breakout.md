You start a new retinoid or exfoliant, then more pimples appear. Is your skin “purging,” or is the product causing a breakout?

Purging is a popular term for a temporary acne flare after starting an ingredient that changes skin-cell turnover. It is not a free pass for months of burning, swelling, or steadily worsening acne.

---

## Signs It May Be Purging

- You recently started a retinoid or exfoliating acid
- Blemishes appear mainly where you normally break out
- They are similar to your usual acne
- The pattern begins to settle over several weeks

Not every active causes a purge, and worsening is not required for a product to work.

## Signs It May Be a Breakout or Irritation

- Bumps appear in completely new areas
- The product is a basic moisturizer or cleanser without a turnover-increasing active
- You have intense itching, burning, swelling, or a rash
- Acne keeps becoming more severe beyond the expected adjustment period
- Your skin feels raw and painful

A reaction can be irritation, an allergy, or acne triggered by a product. Stop and seek medical advice for facial swelling, blistering, breathing difficulty, or a rapidly spreading reaction.

![Calm skin after simplifying an irritating routine](/images/calm-skin-after-purging-routine.png)

## What to Do Next

1. Do not add another active to “fight” the flare.
2. Reduce frequency if the product instructions or your clinician allow it.
3. Use a gentle cleanser, plain moisturizer, and sunscreen.
4. Avoid scrubs, picking, and multiple exfoliating acids.
5. Take weekly photos in the same lighting to judge the trend.

If the flare is severe, scarring, or not clearly improving after about six to eight weeks, ask a dermatologist whether to continue.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can moisturizer cause purging?

A plain moisturizer does not normally speed cell turnover, so new bumps after starting one are unlikely to be purging. The formula may be unsuitable, another product may be responsible, or the timing may be coincidental.

### Does vitamin C cause purging?

Classic vitamin C is not usually considered a purge-causing active. Stinging, rash-like bumps, or worsening redness can point to irritation, the formula's low pH, or another ingredient. Stop if the reaction is significant.

### Should I continue a retinoid through a purge?

Do not make the decision from social media alone. Mild familiar-area acne may be monitored, but painful cysts, scarring, severe irritation, or steadily worsening breakouts need professional advice. Prescription users should contact the prescriber before changing their schedule.

## Final Thoughts

Timing and location offer clues, but there is no perfect home test. A true adjustment should trend toward improvement. Ongoing pain, rash-like symptoms, or worsening breakouts deserve a change of plan—not more patience.
